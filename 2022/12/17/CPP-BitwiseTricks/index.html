Trick1: Check if a number is even or odd

Do not use modular(mod) operators, SLOW!

Check if the least significant bit in the binary representation of a number is one(odd) or not(0, even)

It will take the end of your number with just a single bit which is one, and if you take the end and if it comes out to be one, then you clearly know that the least significant bit is one, so the number is odd. And if it comes out to be zero then you know that you know the least significant bit is zero and the number is even.

Trick3: Playing with the kth bit

When I say kth bit, I am looking at the kth bit from the right to left.

How to check whether the kth bit in a number is set or not?
You take the and of the number with 2 to the power k, if you get a number greater than zero, then there is a bit which was set which is the K bit. If the number comes out to be zero then the kth bit was not set.

Hot wo toggle the kth bit?
You can take a xor with 2 to the power k

How to set the kth bit?
You can take a or with 2 to the power k

How to unset the kth bit?
You can take a and with the not of 2 to the power k

Trick5: Find out X modulo 2 to the power k (X % 2^k)

You can X and 2 to power k minus 1


If you take x and ((1 << k) - 1), you will just look at the last K minus 1 bits, and you will get that from X. The entire part left to the last K minus 1 bits part is already a multiple of 2 to the power k. SO the result you got represent the remainder when you divide x by 2 to the power k
