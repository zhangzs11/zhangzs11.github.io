Skybox Rendering

A skybox is a cube-shaped environment texture that surrounds the entire scene, simulating a far-away background.

Skybox Workflow

The skybox rendering process is performed after drawing the scene’s meshed. It involves:

  1. Vertex transformations : A cube is used as the geometry for the skybox. Transform the cube vertices to match the camera’s perspective without considering depth.
  2. Sampling a cube texture : Use a texture cube (TextureCube) to display the environment.
  3. Combining with scene data : Integrate the skybox with the previously rendered scene, blending it based on depth information.

Vertex Shader

In the VS for skybox, the goal is to create the illusion of an infinitely distant background that moves with the camera’s orientation but remain s unaffected by camera’s position.

VS code:

Transformation 1 : Transform to Camera Space:

The transformation uses only the rotation part (top-left 3x3 submatrix) of the g_transform_worldToCamera matrix to rotate vertex position into camera space.
This ensures the skybox aligns with the camera’s orientation but ignores its translation.

Transformation 2 : Project to Clip Space:

After projecting the vertex position to clip space, the .xyww modification is applied to set z = w.
By doing this, z/w = 1, which effectively places the skybox geometry on the far plane of the view frustum. This ensures that the skybox appears infinitely far away and unaffected by perspective distortion,

Outputs:

  • o_vertexPosition_projected: The final projected position for rasterization.
  • o_vertexPosition_local: The local-space position passed to the pixel shader for sampling the cube texture.

Pixel Shader

The pixel shader combines the rendered scene and the skybox by checking the depth buffer and sampling the cube texture.
PS code:

Input Textures:

  • g_DepthTexture: The depth buffer texture, storing the depth of previously rendered meshes.
  • g_LitTexture: The texture containing the rendered scene colors.
  • g_texture_cube: The cube map texture representing the skybox.

    Blend Skybox and Scene

    Condition : If the depth is 1.0 (indicating no objects were rendered at this fragment), the sky is sampled using the cube texture. The sampling is performed with the following line:
    lit += g_texture_cube.Sample(g_sampler, i_fragmentPosition_local).rgb;
    Sampling : The i_fragmentPosition_local is used as the direction for sampling. It represents the direction from the camera to the cube’s surface.
